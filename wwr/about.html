History

White water rafting is found as far back as 1811. Early explorers attempted to navigate the Snake river in Wyoming. Many died attempting to ride this untamed river. Today its known as the "Mad River".

With the great movement of settlers going west in the 1800s, rafting became more common. Great financial gains could be had for acquiring reliable information for maps and surveys for the many businessmen taking advantage of the boom to go west.
